The Beating Heart of Old Beijing: Everyday Life Inside the Hutongs

Beijing’s hutongs are often described as relics of the past, but they remain vibrant living communities shaped by the people who move through their narrow lanes each day. This photo series captures the quiet rhythms and human connections that define local life: sanitation workers sweeping the alleys, repairmen and scrap collectors keeping small trades alive, elderly residents resting by their doorsteps, and neighbors pause for conversations. On this cold winter morning, sunlight cuts through the alleys, carrying the scent of frying noodles, and the pan sizzling sound. Between these red doors and grey-brick walls, history lingers – but life breathes, bright and present.
